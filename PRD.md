\## SECTION 2 — Problem \& Purpose



\### Problem Statement



Many small businesses, clinics, service providers, and startups understand that they need a stronger digital presence, but they do not clearly understand how websites, web applications, mobile applications, AI tools, and automation can improve their business operations.



They often rely on slow manual processes, scattered communication, weak branding, outdated websites, and disconnected tools. This leads to lost leads, delayed service delivery, poor customer experience, and reduced trust.



\### Project Purpose



The purpose of the Shift Digital Website Platform is to create a professional promotional website for Shift Digital, owned by Dr. Ahmed Awni, a dentist with a passion for development, AI tools, digital transformation, and automation.



The website must communicate that Shift Digital helps businesses move faster, serve customers better, improve operational quality, and adopt smart digital systems through websites, web apps, mobile apps, AI tools, and automation.



\### Business Value



The website must create measurable business value by:



1\. Building trust around Shift Digital and Dr. Ahmed Awni.

2\. Explaining digital transformation in simple business language.

3\. Showing how technology improves speed, quality, and customer experience.

4\. Presenting past and future projects professionally.

5\. Converting visitors into leads through contact forms and direct WhatsApp communication.

6\. Positioning Shift Digital as a practical digital partner for businesses, clinics, startups, and service providers.



\### Opportunity



Business owners increasingly need fast, high-quality digital solutions. Shift Digital can stand out by combining business understanding, healthcare experience, web and mobile development, AI tools, and automation into one clear offer.



The website is the first public platform for promoting Shift Digital and converting interest into client conversations.

\### P0-F001 — Homepage Hero



\#### Description



The homepage hero must immediately explain who Shift Digital is, what it offers, and why digital transformation matters for business speed and quality.



\#### User Story



As a business owner, I want to understand Shift Digital’s value within the first few seconds so that I can decide whether to continue exploring the website.



\#### Trigger



User opens the website homepage.



\#### Pre-conditions



1\. Website is deployed and accessible.

2\. Homepage content is available.

3\. User has an active internet connection.



\#### Post-conditions



1\. User sees Shift Digital’s main value proposition.

2\. User can click a primary CTA to contact Shift Digital.

3\. User can click a secondary CTA to view projects or services.



\#### Main Flow



1\. User opens the website.

2\. System loads the homepage hero.

3\. System displays brand name, headline, subheadline, and CTA buttons.

4\. User reads the value proposition.

5\. User clicks “Start Your Digital Shift” or “View Projects.”

6\. System scrolls to the contact section or portfolio section.

7\. User continues browsing or submits an inquiry.



\#### Alternate Flows



1\. If the hero image fails to load, the system displays the text content and fallback background.

2\. If the CTA target section fails to load, the system keeps the user on the page and shows a non-blocking error message.

3\. If the user is on mobile, the hero layout stacks vertically and CTA buttons remain visible.



\#### Acceptance Criteria



\- \[ ] Hero section displays Shift Digital brand name.

\- \[ ] Hero section includes a clear headline about digital transformation.

\- \[ ] Hero section includes a CTA for contact.

\- \[ ] Hero section includes a CTA for projects or services.

\- \[ ] Hero section loads in less than 2 seconds on standard broadband.

\- \[ ] Hero section is fully responsive on mobile, tablet, and desktop.

\- \[ ] CTA buttons show loading or scroll feedback when clicked.

\- \[ ] Broken images do not break the layout.

User clicks a contact CTA or scrolls to the contact section.

\#### Acceptance Criteria



\- \[ ] Form includes name field.

\- \[ ] Form includes email or phone field.

\- \[ ] Form includes service interest field.

\- \[ ] Form includes message field.

\- \[ ] Required fields validate before submission.

\- \[ ] Invalid inputs show inline errors.

\- \[ ] Lead data is sent to the configured destination.

\## SECTION 11 — Advanced Execution Rules



\### User Flow



1\. Visitor opens the Shift Digital website from search, social media, referral, or direct link.

2\. System loads the homepage hero with brand message and CTAs.

3\. Visitor reads that Shift Digital helps businesses improve speed, quality, automation, and digital growth.

4\. Visitor clicks “Explore Services.”

5\. System scrolls to services section.

6\. Visitor reviews websites, web apps, mobile apps, AI tools, automation, and digital systems.

7\. Visitor opens the digital transformation section.

8\. System explains business benefits using simple examples.

9\. Visitor opens the project portfolio.

10\. System displays project cards and outcomes.

11\. Visitor clicks a project to see more details.

12\. System expands the project card or opens a detail modal.

13\. Visitor clicks “Start Your Digital Shift.”

14\. System scrolls to contact form.

15\. Visitor enters name, email or phone, service interest, business type, and message.

16\. System validates input instantly.

17\. Visitor submits the form.

18\. System displays loading state.

19\. System sends the inquiry.

20\. System displays success confirmation.

21\. Shift Digital receives the lead and can follow up.



\### Edge Cases



| Condition | System Behavior | User Feedback |

|---|---|---|

| Required contact field is empty | Block submission and highlight field | “Please enter your name.” |

| Email format is invalid | Block submission | “Please enter a valid email address.” |

| Phone format is invalid | Block submission | “Please enter a valid phone number.” |

| Both email and phone are missing | Block submission | “Please provide an email or phone number so we can contact you.” |

| Message is too short | Block submission | “Please describe your project in at least 20 characters.” |

| Network failure during form submission | Preserve form data and allow retry | “Connection problem. Please try again or contact us on WhatsApp.” |

| Backend API failure | Log error and show fallback | “Something went wrong. You can still contact us on WhatsApp.” |

| Portfolio has no projects | Display empty state | “Selected projects are being prepared. Contact us to discuss your project.” |

| Duplicate rapid form submission | Prevent second submission | “Your request was already submitted.” |

| WhatsApp link fails | Show fallback phone number | “WhatsApp could not open. Please use this number: \[TBD].” |

| Unauthorized admin access in future CMS | Block access | “You do not have permission to access this area.” |

| Analytics script blocked | Website continues normally | No user-facing message required |

\### UX Rules

7\. Mobile navigation must be reachable with one tap.

8\. Website must not rely on animations to explain important content.

9\. Every section must have a clear heading and business purpose.

10\. Users must always have a visible next action.
